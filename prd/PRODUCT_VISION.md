# Product Vision

## Vision

Build the operating system for automated print-on-demand manufacturing and fulfillment.

The platform allows online sellers to create products, generate mockups, connect their stores, publish products, receive orders, pay through prepaid balance, track fulfillment, and manage returned inventory from one dashboard.

## Product Positioning

The product is not a normal print shop. It is fulfillment infrastructure for online sellers.

The seller experience should be simple:

1. Create an account.
2. Connect Shopify or WooCommerce.
3. Select a product.
4. Upload a design.
5. Preview mockups.
6. Set pricing.
7. Publish to the store.
8. Receive orders automatically.
9. Pay supplier charges through prepaid wallet.
10. Track production, shipping, delivery, and returns.

## Target Users

Primary users:

- Shopify sellers.
- WooCommerce sellers.
- Dropshipping sellers.
- Small merchandise brands.
- Influencers launching merchandise.
- Agencies managing online stores.
- Online sellers testing new designs.

Internal users:

- Platform admin.
- Seller support team.
- Product catalog manager.
- Printing staff.
- Quality control staff.
- Packing staff.
- Dispatch staff.
- Finance team.
- Operations manager.

## Main Promise

A seller should be able to launch a product in less than 10 minutes and fulfill future orders without manual supplier coordination.

The seller should not need to manually send designs, addresses, order details, packing details, or tracking requests.

## Business Model

The platform earns from product margin, shipping margin, optional subscriptions, premium tools, warehousing fees, and advanced API access.

## Distribution Strategy

The main growth channel is partnerships with e-commerce educators, online business communities, and seller communities. These partners already create new sellers. The platform should become their recommended fulfillment partner.

The system must support tracking seller source, partner code, activation, first order, wallet recharge, and partner commission reports.

## Differentiators

- Seller-friendly product creation.
- Realistic mockup generation.
- Shopify and WooCommerce publishing.
- Prepaid supplier billing.
- Live production tracking.
- Barcode-based factory workflow.
- Return-to-inventory workflow.
- Simple dashboard for non-technical sellers.
- Automation-first operations.
- Future multi-factory support.

## North Star Metric

Number of active sellers receiving at least one order per day.

## Supporting Metrics

- Seller activation rate.
- Store connection success rate.
- Product publish success rate.
- First-order rate.
- Orders per active seller per day.
- Wallet recharge success rate.
- Production SLA compliance.
- Dispatch SLA compliance.
- Return/RTO rate.
- Packing error rate.
- Seller retention.
- Gross margin per order.
- Daily order volume.
- Factory utilization rate.

## Long-Term Vision

The system should eventually support multiple product categories, multiple factories, multiple shipping partners, multiple sales channels, mobile apps, public APIs, design marketplace, AI product tools, warehousing, and B2B bulk merchandise orders.

## Design Philosophy

The seller portal must be modern, clean, and simple.

The factory portal must be practical, large, readable, and fast for staff near machines and packing stations.

The admin portal must focus on money, bottlenecks, exceptions, seller health, and operational control.
